RAC Overview
Mission & Value

RAC’s mission is to foster a network of entrepreneurial support within the Rice community. Through strategic partnerships, mentorship, and access to resources, RAC provides what alumni entrepreneurs need to flourish while also giving alumni investors and partners meaningful opportunities for growth and impact.

  • Rice Excellence

    For the fifth consecutive year, Rice University's Jones Graduate School of Business has been ranked No. 1 for graduate entrepreneurship programs by Princeton Review and Entrepreneur magazine (2023).

  • Rice Alumni Entrepreneurs

    Utilizing Rice University’s strong network and community, RAC’s primary mission is to support Rice Alumni entrepreneurs as they work to acquire and operate successful businesses.

  • Rice Alumni Investors

    The RAC Fund provides Alumni investors a means to support the Rice entrepreneur community while offering exclusive access to investments targeting upper-tier returns (20%+ IRR).

  • Strategic External Partnerships

    As the hub for this entrepreneurial community, RAC has created a premier network of external capital providers. These connections make it possible to activate resources and capabilities that extend beyond the capacity of a RAC fund.

RAC History

The Rice Alumni Capital Fund was created from decades of educational and professional experience of Rice University alumni/entrepreneurs who made the system work.

  • Foundational Achievements

    Ed Williams, a successful entrepreneur, helped his high school friend, Robert Waltrip, take Service Corporation International (SCI) public. Through Williams’ strategic “rollup” approach, they consolidated smaller companies into a single parent company and grew SCI into the largest in its industry.

    1970

  • Jones School Entrepreneurship Program

    Ed Williams (age 33) founded the Jones School Entrepreneurship Program at Rice University -- an early pioneer in the field as there were only a few graduate programs focused on entrepreneurshipat the time.

    1978

  • Growth and Influence

    Ed Williams was instrumental in establishing the Rice Business Plan Competition, now the world’s largest (and richest) business plan competition for graduate schools.

    (Jones School students have awarded Williams the Teaching Excellent Award seven times.)

    1980

  • MBA programs at Rice University

    While concurrently running a computer consulting and education business (for 21 years), Professor Al Napier taught the New Enterprise course across MBA programs at Rice University. Napier was known for his research in entrepreneurship and human/computer interaction, authoring sixteen books on the subject and publishing more than fifty scholarly articles.

    1990

  • New Leadership and Initiatives

    Al Danto came to Rice University’s MBA program having already built Structured Graphics, a company recognized in printing and document management. As an MBA student, he started Universal Worker as a class project.

    2000

  • Entrepreneurship initiatives at Rice University

    After graduating, Al Danto collaborated with Ed Williams and Al Napier on several important projects including the Rice Alumni Entrepreneurs Organization, student and alumni pitch competitions (which became the Napier Launch Challenge), the Owl Spark Summer Accelerator, E-Labs and Alumni Roundtables.

    2010

  • Transition and Expansion

    Ed Williams and Al Napier began to transition their roles and responsibilities to Al Danto while continuing their work in entrepreneurship initiatives.

    2012

  • Innovation and Entrepreneurship

    Present Day: The work of these three individuals – and countless others – has culminated in the Liu Idea Lab for Innovation and Entrepreneurship (LILIE), a central hub for experiential learning and co-curricular activities in entrepreneurship and innovation. LILIE offers a range of undergraduate and graduate courses and -- in collaboration with Rice Business, the George R. Brown School of Engineering, and the School of Social Sciences -- now offers a new minor in entrepreneurship. LILIE’s mission is to prepare Rice students with an entrepreneurial mindset and skills applicable across myriad industries.

    2015

  • RAC Fund I (Rice Alumni Capital)

    RAC (Rice Alumni Capital) was launched, with Al Danto and other leaders from Rice’s entrepreneur program serving as Advisors. The Fund is managed by TEI (Texas Essential Investments), a Rice Alumni led private investment group. RAC’s primary mission is to support Rice Alumni entrepreneurs in successfully acquiring and operating businesses.

    2024

Ideal RAC Investment

Rice alumni are regularly acquiring existing businesses where they can use their talents to create transformative growth and value. RAC Fund I seeks to support aspiring Rice entrepreneurs and provide capital to the best of these opportunities.

A Sample Target Investment
  • Industry: healthcare, business services, consumer/retail, technology, industrials/manufacturing, education, and financial services
  • Business Profile: Established, valued enterprise with strong customer base
  • Seller Profile: Baby boomer that is looking to retire
  • Buyer Profile: Rice Alumni known and trusted by the network
  • EBITDA: $1-5 Million / year
  • Exit: 3 to 5 years
  • Terms: Preferred rate (8-15%) with waterfall structure targeting 20%+ annual net returns
Fund Summary
  • RAC Fund I Target: $1.0 Million, in 4 to 5 Alumni Investments
  • Expected Investment Period: 1 year (2025)
  • Expected Hold Period: 3 to 5 years
  • Target Investment IRR: 20%+
Investor Terms
  • Minimum Alumni Investor Contribution: $25,000 Capital called twice at $12,500 ea. First call – Q4 2024
  • Preferred Return: 10%
Minimal Fund Overhead to Maximize Alumni Returns
  • 1% Annual Fund Mgmt Fee on unreturned committed capital Waterfall
  • 100% LP Investor / 0% GP up to the 10% preferred return
  • 85% LP Investor / 15% GP thereafter

RAC Advisors
Don Porr

Don earned a B.S. in Mechanical Engineering from Tulsa University (’92) and a Rice MBA (’97). An early member of Rice’s Jones Graduate School Entrepreneurs’ Organization, he now participates as a member of JGSEO’s successor, the Rice Alumni Entrepreneurs and Innovators group. Through this alumni organization, Don is a 12-year member of a CEO Roundtable, advising other members and CEOs on startups, failures, business acquisitions and business harvests.

Additionally, Don is a guest lecturer and instructor in entrepreneurship at Rice Business where he currently teaches both enterprise acquisition and enterprise acquisition e-lab courses for 2nd-year MBA students. When not working, Don enjoys entrepreneurship advising and investing, broadening his world perspective by traveling with his wife and family, cycling with friends and tinkering on classic vehicles.

RAC Advisors
Professor Al Danto

In 2012, Al Napier and Ed Williams began passing their torch to Al Danto, a Rice MBA who had founded, grown and exited Structured Graphics, through a series of acquisitions into a nationally recognized printing and document management company. Al also started Universal Worker, an international healthcare staffing company, as a class project while an MBA student.

Shortly after graduating from Rice in 2000, Al became a regular guest speaker and worked closely with Ed and Al on several entrepreneurship initiatives. These included the Rice Alumni Entrepreneurs Organization, the student and alumni pitch competition (now the Napier Launch Challenge), The Owl Spark summer Accelerator, E-Labs (where students earn credit while starting their own businesses or beginning the process to acquire a business), and Alumni Roundtables, where alumni meet in small groups to support each other’s entrepreneurial initiatives.

RAC Fund I Investment Committee
Steve Reis

“I’m a part of RAC because I was able to more substantially contribute to my community and family when investors entrusted me with their capital and support, and I want to help up-and- coming entrepreneurs get that same opportunity.”

After growing up in south Florida, Steve Reis graduated from The Citadel and joined the US Army as an Infantry Officer in the 82nd Airborne Division. While serving he led a platoon of Paratroopers in Afghanistan and was later second-in-command of an Infantry company. He left the Army shortly thereafter and began a new career in the energy industry by joining EnerMech Mechanical Services, a fast-growing oilfield service company led by a group of seasoned entrepreneurs.

His time there involved improving business infrastructure systems, integrating an acquired company into the corporation, and finally, standing-up a new business unit while beginning his MBA studies at Rice University. In 2019 Mr. Reis led a group of investors to acquire Holland Supply Company, a value-added reseller of utility technologies. He took over management of the company as CEO and later led the formation of a vertically integrated utility technology manufacturer, Utility Solutions Group. Mr. Reis serves as CEO of both companies today and lives in Ohio with his wife and daughter, Natalie and Olivia.

RAC Fund I Investment Committee
Kory Li

“None of us is as smart as all of us”

Kory Li is a Principal at Siltstone Capital and Board Director of Clean Royalties. Prior to Siltstone, Mr. Li was an Associate Director at UBS Investment Bank covering both traditional energy and the broader energy transition. While at UBS, he accumulated over $3 billion worth of transaction experience ranging from SPAC/deSPACs, private capital raises, and strategic M&A.

Mr. Li was instrumental in the launch and buildout of the Houston-based energy transition coverage team, covering the battery value chain, renewable fuels, and utility-scale wind and solar. Mr. Li previously held roles in increasing seniority at Anheuser-Busch Inbev ranging from M&A to operations to business development. Mr. Li received his B.S. in chemical engineering and finance from Washington University in St. Louis and an MBA from Rice University.
